Architectural Design: Stairs

For this project, I designed stairs for a single-family home with the main access starting at the ground floor and extending to the first floor.

Previous Project Next Project

The ground floor structure is approximately 6.3″ high
The floor structure on the gallery on the upper floor consists of a wooden beam construction with a 1″ multiplex covering.

The second structure is approximately 3″ from the top edge of the Multiplex.
For this project, I provided detailed plans for constructing a single-flight staircase, including railing and gallery railing.
Comprehensiveness and detail played an essential role in guaranteeing the functionality and adhere to architectural standards and regulations.

I planned a cheek staircase (cheek thickness 3/4″, flat steel, lacquered) with
steps made of solid wood (beech, thickness 1 1/2″) and a stainless steel railing (welded and screwed).
